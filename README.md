# Project Scumpy  
## High-Performance Workstation Architecture Research Case Study

## Executive Summary
This case study evaluates the design of a hypothetical high-performance gaming and streaming workstation for a client scenario involving OpTic Gaming.

The objective was to recommend a hardware architecture capable of meeting performance goals for competitive gaming, content creation, and low-latency streaming while operating within an approximate $5,000 budget.

The proposed solution emphasizes:
- Compute performance
- Graphics throughput
- Thermal stability
- Component compatibility
- Upgrade longevity

**Note**: This is a research and design case study and does not represent a physically assembled or benchmarked system.

---

## Problem Statement
The client required a workstation capable of:

- Sustained high FPS gaming performance
- Simultaneous streaming capability
- Low-latency operation
- High-speed storage performance
- Future-proof platform compatibility

Target completion date and budget constraints were defined during intake.  

---

## Constraints
The solution was developed under the following constraints:

### Budget Constraint
Target budget:
- $5,000 USD

Final estimated cost:
- $4,972 pre-tax
- $5,182.20 after tax

---

## Technical Constraints
Selections had to consider:

- CPU/GPU balance
- PCIe and memory compatibility
- Power delivery requirements
- Cooling requirements
- Physical component fitment
- Platform upgrade path

---

## Methodology
Hardware selection was based on:

1. Vendor specifications  
2. Compatibility analysis  
3. Cost-performance tradeoff analysis  
4. Published performance characteristics  
5. Risk reduction through future-proof component selection

---

## Proposed Architecture

### Compute Layer
**CPU:** AMD Ryzen 9 9950X3D

Selection rationale:
- High core count
- 3D V-Cache advantage
- Strong gaming and multitasking performance

---

### Graphics Layer
**GPU:** Gigabyte GeForce RTX 5090

Selection rationale:
- High-end rendering capability
- Streaming and gaming workload support
- Long-term performance headroom

---

### Memory and Storage
- 32GB DDR5-6000
- Samsung 990 Pro 2TB NVMe

Selection rationale:
- Low latency memory performance
- High throughput storage subsystem

---

### Platform and Supporting Systems
- MSI X870E Motherboard
- Corsair HX1200i PSU
- iCUE LINK TITAN 360 AIO
- NZXT H9 Flow chassis

Selection rationale:
- Stable power delivery
- Thermal management
- Upgrade flexibility

---

## Tradeoff Analysis

### Tradeoff 1: Performance vs Cost
Higher-end components increased system cost, but improved longevity and reduced performance compromise.

### Tradeoff 2: Cooling vs Simplicity
AIO cooling increased complexity but reduced thermal risk.

### Tradeoff 3: Premium Components vs Budget Target
The design slightly exceeded budget after tax, but remained aligned with performance objectives.

---

## Risk Considerations
Potential risks evaluated:

- Power draw requirements
- Thermal bottlenecks
- Component pricing volatility
- Future workload scaling

Mitigation:
- Oversized PSU
- High-capacity cooling solution
- Platform with upgrade headroom

This research reinforced the importance of:

- Balanced system design
